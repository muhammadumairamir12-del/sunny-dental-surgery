Common Dental Conditions We Treat

Recognising dental diseases early can prevent pain, tooth loss, and costly treatments. Learn the warning signs below.

Moderate Risk

Tooth Decay (Caries)

Dental caries is the breakdown of tooth enamel caused by acids produced by bacteria. It's the most common dental disease worldwide and progresses silently if untreated.

Toothache Sensitivity Dark Spots Holes in Teeth
High Risk

Gum Disease

Periodontal disease ranges from mild gingivitis to advanced periodontitis. Untreated gum disease is the leading cause of tooth loss in adults and has links to systemic health issues.

Bleeding Gums Swelling Bad Breath Receding Gums
Urgent Care

Dental Abscess

A dental abscess is a pocket of pus caused by bacterial infection, typically at the root tip or in the gum. It requires immediate treatment to prevent the infection from spreading.

Severe Pain Swelling Fever Pus / Foul Taste
Preventable

Dental Plaque & Tartar

Plaque is a sticky bacterial film that forms on teeth daily. When not removed, it hardens into tartar — a major contributor to both cavities and gum disease.

Yellow Build-up Rough Texture Bad Breath Gum Irritation
